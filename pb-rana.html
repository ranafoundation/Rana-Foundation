Rana P.B. SINGH

Indian geographer

Professor Rana P.B. Singh is a distinguished scholar in the fields of Cultural Geography, Sacred Geography, Pilgrimage Studies, and Heritage Planning in India. He is a Professor at Banaras Hindu University (BHU), Varanasi, where he has made significant contributions to geographical thought and heritage studies.

With a prolific academic career, Prof. Singh has authored or edited 35 books and published over 180 research papers in national and international journals. His research primarily focuses on the spiritual landscapes of India, particularly the sacred geography of Varanasi, a city he has extensively studied and written about. His works explore themes such as Hindu cosmology, ritual spaces, pilgrimage traditions, and urban heritage conservation.

As a leading expert in the geography of religion, he has engaged in interdisciplinary research, incorporating perspectives from history, anthropology, and environmental studies to understand the deep-rooted connections between landscapes and spiritual traditions. His work has been influential in shaping the discourse on pilgrimage and sacred sites in South Asia, earning him recognition among scholars worldwide.

Prof. Singh's dedication to heritage planning extends to his active involvement in cultural conservation projects and advocacy for the sustainable management of historic cities like Varanasi. His contributions have helped bridge the gap between traditional knowledge systems and modern urban planning.

Throughout his career, he has mentored scholars and researchers, fostering a deeper understanding of India's sacred landscapes and cultural traditions. His academic influence extends beyond India, with numerous collaborations with international researchers and institutions focusing on pilgrimage studies and religious landscapes.

Prof. Rana P.B. Singh remains a pioneering figure in cultural geography, continuously contributing to the preservation and academic study of India's sacred heritage and ritual spaces.
